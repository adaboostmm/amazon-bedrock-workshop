# Papers Focusing on MEV at SBC 2025

Based on the conference program, here are the papers that **directly or closely relate to MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)**:

---

## ?? Directly MEV-Focused Papers

### 1. **"The Early Days of the Ethereum Blob Fee Market and Lessons Learnt"**
- **Session 6:** Blobs in Ethereum
- **Authors:** Lioba Heimbach (ETH Zurich), Jason Milionis (Columbia University)
- **MEV Relevance:** Blob fee markets create **new MEV opportunities** in how L2 sequencers post data to L1. Understanding blob fee dynamics is critical for **block builders and searchers** extracting value from L2 batch submissions.

---

### 2. **"Sak: Time to be Selffes?! Demystifying the Landscape of Selfish Mining Strategies and Models"**
- **Session 9:** Consensus II
- **Authors:** Colin Finkbeiner, Mohamed E. Najd (University of Connecticut); Julia Guskind (Boston University); Ghada Almashaqbeh (University of Connecticut)
- **MEV Relevance:** Selfish mining is a form of **consensus-level MEV extraction** where miners/validators strategically withhold blocks to gain disproportionate rewards. This directly relates to **MEV at the consensus layer**.

---

### 3. **"Good Things Come To Those Who Wait: Dishonest Majority Coin Flipping Requires Delay Functions"**
- **Session 9:** Consensus II
- **Authors:** Juan Garay (NYU & a]6 crypto); Bene?t Buro (UTU); Miranda Christ, Yuval Efron (Columbia University)
- **MEV Relevance:** Delay functions and fair ordering are **key MEV mitigation techniques**. This research addresses how to prevent manipulation in randomness generation, which is connected to **fair transaction ordering**.

---

### 4. **"Quantifying the Value of Revert Protection"**
- **Session 13:** Mechanism Design
- **Authors:** Brian Z. Zhu (Columbia University); Xin Wan (Uniswap Labs); Ciamac C. Moallemi (Columbia University / Paradigm / Uniswap Labs); Dan Robinson (Paradigm); Brad Bachu (Uniswap Labs)
- **MEV Relevance:** **Directly MEV-focused.** Revert protection is a core feature of **Flashbots Protect and MEV Blocker**. This paper quantifies the economic value users gain from being protected against **failed transactions caused by MEV bots** (sandwich attacks, front-running). The authorship from **Paradigm and Uniswap Labs** signals direct industry application.

---

### 5. **"Latency Advantages in Common Value Auctions"**
- **Session 13:** Mechanism Design
- **Authors:** Ciamac C. Moallemi (Columbia University / Paradigm / Uniswap Labs); Mallesh M Pai (Consensus / Paradigm); Dan Robinson (Paradigm)
- **MEV Relevance:** **Directly MEV-focused.** Latency advantages are at the **heart of MEV extraction** — faster actors can front-run, back-run, and sandwich slower participants. This paper analyzes auction mechanisms (relevant to **MEV auctions, Flashbots SUAVE, and Proposer-Builder Separation**). Again authored by **Paradigm** researchers who are leading MEV research.

---

### 6. **"LeveragingConomic Recovery from Manipulated Signals, with Applications to Decentralized Physical Infrastructure"**
- **Session 13:** Mechanism Design
- **Authors:** Jason Milionis (Columbia University); Anu Energizer (Technical University of Munich); Joseph Bonneau (NYU & a16z crypto); Scott Duke Kominers (Harvard University & a16z crypto)
- **MEV Relevance:** Signal manipulation and economic recovery mechanisms relate to **MEV in broader decentralized systems** — including how manipulated price oracles and signals can be exploited for extractable value.

---

### 7. **"Invited: The Evolving Art of Agreement: Post-Blockchain Theory and Practice of Consensus Protocols"**
- **Session 9:** Consensus II
- **Authors:** Aggelos Kiayias (University of Edinburgh and IOG)
- **MEV Relevance:** Consensus protocol design has **deep implications for MEV** — how transactions are ordered, who gets to propose blocks, and what fairness guarantees exist are all consensus-level decisions that determine MEV opportunities.

---

### 8. **"No Fish Is Too Big for Flash Boys! Frontrunning on DAG-based Blockchains"**
- **Session 9:** Consensus II
- **Authors:** Jianting Zhang (Purdue University); Anisht Kate (Purdue University / Supra Research)
- **MEV Relevance:** **Directly MEV-focused.** The title "Flash Boys" is a direct reference to **MEV/front-running**. This paper examines how front-running (a primary form of MEV) manifests in **DAG-based blockchains** like Sui, IOTA, and similar systems — extending MEV research beyond traditional linear blockchains.
